LUTEAL PHASE

What is LUTEAL phase ? This is the second half of your menstrual circle after ovulation The luteal phase is when the egg starts traveling down the Fallopian tube ,which ends when your period begins ,this averagely lasts 12 to 14 days for most women,it’s also longer or shorter for some women.

A long Luteal phase may be due to hormonal imbalance or it could mean you are pregnant but you just haven’t realized it.A short luteal phase may be a result of luteal phase defect ,this means the ovaries produces less progesterone than it should ,this might lead to infertility or miscarriage. Certain lifestyle e.g smoking might also lead to less progesterone counts.

The last week before period is the most difficult time for majority of females ,a lot of things can happen during this time.
I personally experience symptoms like brain fog ; I tend to forget things or have to think really hard when explaining simple things. nausea,bloating( if my butt would bloat as much as my stomach I would be a happy girl) anxiety; I tend to overthink and easily break down,being cold or sweaty,extreme fatigue; zero motivation to do any sort of physical activities.Hasten sense of smell ; I tend to be irritated by my own smell , leave a lone another persons smell. During this period I just don’t feel like hanging out with people or having a social life because of extreme low moods .

The solution to this symptoms is to tune in to your body and find out why you are getting this symptoms .
In my case for example I suffer from low blood pressure,lactose and gluten intolerance, hypersensitivity to acid. I’m sure you are wondering how all of this is related to my luteal phase , let me break it down to you real quick.

Estrogen levels rise during your pms week causing blood pressure to drop,if your blood pressure is already low you can imagine the end results ,dizziness and fatigue are heightened.
If you are gluten intolerant,every time you eat gluten you cause inflammation in your body ,which could contribute to lots of other disorders eg heart disease, diabetes,arthritis . Gluten not only messes with the hormone production in my body but also causes a total destruction in the gut .In addition to being gluten intolerant I’m also lactose intolerant and any additional source of estrogen (dairy,soy) during luteal phase affects me way more than my non gluten sensitive friends.You get the picture .

As a person who’s so much into fitness, I try to get most of the work done in the follicular phase when my energy is at its best so that I can ease up towards my PMS because I know a week before my period,my motivation is going to be pretty low .There are high chances of slow muscle growth,slow recovery, less pain tolerance because of the drop in estrogen and progesterone levels.
Our bodies requires 200-300 more calories during this time because our basal metabolic rate(the amount of energy you spend at rest )is slightly higher ,the week leading to your period you burn more calories than any other time of the month,this is why we tend to get hungrier during this time.
Period related weight gain is as a result of hormonal fluctuations causing water retention ,sugar cravings and skipping workouts due to cramps ,it’s very common for the scale to go up even when you are actually not gaining fats.
During week 3 Your body doesn’t tolerate carbs because you are more insulin resistant ( inability of cells to respond to insulin in the action of transportation of glucose from the blood stream to the muscle and other tissues) dopamine and serotonin (your feel good mood) drops .

I don’t believe that we are victims of our hormones to blame it all on them, we can have personal accountability to keep things in perspective so that you kinda know where your body is at.

It’s advisable to start your diet during follicular face to help counteract insulin domination .If you are a woman looking to start your diet during luteal phase you might be setting yourself up to failure. In my next blog I’ll be writing about the nutritional guidelines during luteal phase .
Just to be clear,I’m not a doctor but I’m writing according to my research,personal experience and the experience of the people surrounding me .
